MEET ADA LOVELACE

Almost 200 years ago, Ada Lovelace wrote the first computer program in the world even before computers existed! She imagined machines capable of finding hidden patterns in data.

Today, well follow in her footsteps using our pocket computer the micro:bit. Well program it to measure:

Light: to see how brightness decreases when the Moon covers the Sun.
Temperature: to observe whether it drops during the eclipse.
Ambient sound: to check if the environment becomes quieter or changes.

When the eclipse arrives, your micro:bit will act as a mini observatory, recording real data every few seconds. Afterwards, well make graphs and identify the key moments (start, maximum, and end or totality, if there is one), just like real scientists!

Are you ready to become eclipse detectives and follow Ada Lovelaces example?
Lets connect, code, and launch our pocket observatories!
